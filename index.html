Aim

The aim of this project is to study the production of biochar from farm wastes and to understand its importance in sustainable agriculture and environmental protection.

Theory

Biochar is a carbon-rich solid obtained when biomass such as agricultural waste is heated in a limited or no oxygen environment. This process is known as pyrolysis. During pyrolysis, volatile components are removed and stable carbon structures are formed.

Biochar improves soil properties, increases nutrient retention, and helps in carbon sequestration, thereby reducing climate change effects.

Materials Required

  • Farm wastes (straw, husk, crop residues)
  • Metal drum or biochar kiln
  • Heat source
  • Protective gloves
  • Water for cooling

Procedure

  1. Collect and dry the farm waste materials.
  2. Place the dried biomass inside a metal drum.
  3. Heat the drum in a low-oxygen environment.
  4. Allow the material to undergo pyrolysis.
  5. After completion, cool the drum using water.
  6. Collect, crush, and store the biochar.
